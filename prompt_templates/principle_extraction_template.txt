You are given a math solution trace.

Your job is to extract a small set of high-level reusable principles that are genuinely supported by the trace.

A high-level principle is:
- abstract enough to transfer to similar problems,
- specific enough to be operational,
- justified by steps that actually appear in the trace.

A bad principle is:
- too generic (“use algebra”),
- not evidenced by the trace,
- broader than what the trace supports.

Requirements:
1. Produce 2 to 5 principles only.
2. Each note must describe one reusable mathematical principle, not a step-by-step summary.
3. The description must say:
   - what the principle is,
   - when it applies,
   - why it works.
4. Keep the wording compact and technical.
5. Do not invent principles not supported by the trace.
6. Prefer principles that are as general as possible while still being faithful to the trace.
7. If two candidate principles overlap heavily, merge them.

Important faithfulness rule:
- Extract principles only from reasoning patterns explicitly used in the trace.
- Do not include unrelated advanced tools unless the trace uses them.
