Problem:
Solve for $r$: $\frac{r+9}{r-3} = \frac{r-2}{r+5}$

Solution:
To solve the equation:

$$
\frac{r + 9}{r - 3} = \frac{r - 2}{r + 5}
$$

---

### Step 1: Cross-Multiply

Since the two fractions are equal, we can cross-multiply:

$$
(r + 9)(r + 5) = (r - 3)(r - 2)
$$

---

### Step 2: Expand Both Sides

**Left-hand side:**

$$
(r + 9)(r + 5) = r^2 + 5r + 9r + 45 = r^2 + 14r + 45
$$

**Right-hand side:**

$$
(r - 3)(r - 2) = r^2 - 2r - 3r + 6 = r^2 - 5r + 6
$$

---

### Step 3: Set the Two Expressions Equal

$$
r^2 + 14r + 45 = r^2 - 5r + 6
$$

Subtract $ r^2 $ from both sides:

$$
14r + 45 = -5r + 6
$$

---

### Step 4: Solve for $ r $

Bring all terms to one side:

$$
14r + 5r + 45 - 6 = 0 \Rightarrow 19r + 39 = 0
$$

Solve for $ r $:

$$
19r = -39 \Rightarrow r = -\frac{39}{19}
$$

---

### Step 5: Verify the Solution

We must ensure that the solution does not make any denominator zero in the original equation.

- $ r - 3 \neq 0 \Rightarrow r \neq 3 $
- $ r + 5 \neq 0 \Rightarrow r \neq -5 $

Since $ r = -\frac{39}{19} \approx -2.05 $, neither of these values is a problem.

Now, substitute $ r = -\frac{39}{19} $ back into the original equation and verify both sides are equal.

After substitution and simplification, both sides are equal, confirming the solution is valid.

---

### Final Answer

$$
\boxed{-\dfrac{39}{19}}
$$

Problem:
Given the definition: $min\\{a,b\\} = \\begin{cases} a,\\quad a\\leqslant b \\\\ b,\\quad a > b \\end{cases}$. If a point $P(x,y)$ is randomly selected within the region defined by $\\begin{cases} 0\\leqslant x\\leqslant 2 \\\\ 0\\leqslant y\\leqslant 6 \\end{cases}$, find the probability that $x$ and $y$ satisfy $min\\{3x-2y+6,x-y+4\\}=x-y+4$.

Solution:
To determine the probability that a randomly selected point $ P(x, y) $ within the rectangle $ 0 \leq x \leq 2 $ and $ 0 \leq y \leq 6 $ satisfies the condition:

$$
\min\{3x - 2y + 6, x - y + 4\} = x - y + 4,
$$

we begin by analyzing the inequality that must hold for the condition to be true.

---

### Step 1: Understand the Condition

The function $ \min\{a, b\} = a $ if $ a \leq b $, and $ \min\{a, b\} = b $ if $ a > b $. So, the condition $ \min\{3x - 2y + 6, x - y + 4\} = x - y + 4 $ implies:

$$
x - y + 4 \leq 3x - 2y + 6
$$

Solving this inequality:

$$
x - y + 4 \leq 3x - 2y + 6
$$

Subtract $ x $ and add $ y $ to both sides:

$$
4 \leq 2x - y + 6
$$

Subtract 6 from both sides:

$$
-2 \leq 2x - y
$$

Rearranging:

$$
2x - y \geq -2
\Rightarrow y \leq 2x + 2
$$

---

### Step 2: Determine the Region of Interest

We are to find the area of the region in the rectangle $ [0, 2] \times [0, 6] $ where $ y \leq 2x + 2 $.

The line $ y = 2x + 2 $ intersects the rectangle at two points:

- When $ x = 0 $, $ y = 2 $ → point $ (0, 2) $
- When $ y = 6 $, $ 6 = 2x + 2 \Rightarrow x = 2 $ → point $ (2, 6) $

So, the line goes from $ (0, 2) $ to $ (2, 6) $, and the region $ y \leq 2x + 2 $ is the area below this line within the rectangle.

---

### Step 3: Compute the Area of the Region

We compute the area under the line $ y = 2x + 2 $ from $ x = 0 $ to $ x = 2 $, which is the integral:

$$
\int_0^2 (2x + 2) \, dx = \left[ x^2 + 2x \right]_0^2 = (4 + 4) - (0 + 0) = 8
$$

So, the area where $ y \leq 2x + 2 $ is **8 square units**.

---

### Step 4: Compute the Total Area of the Rectangle

The rectangle has width $ 2 $ and height $ 6 $, so its total area is:

$$
2 \times 6 = 12
$$

---

### Step 5: Compute the Probability

The probability that a randomly selected point satisfies the condition is the ratio of the favorable area to the total area:

$$
\text{Probability} = \frac{8}{12} = \frac{2}{3}
$$

---

### Final Answer

$$
\boxed{\dfrac{2}{3}}
$$
