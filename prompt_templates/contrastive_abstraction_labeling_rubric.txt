Contrastive Abstraction Labeling Rubric

Primary objective:
- Increase downstream solve probability when the abstraction is used as a hint.

Required properties:
- Structural: identifies the decisive representation, invariant, decomposition, construction, or reduction.
- Operational: tells the solver what to do, not just what topic the problem belongs to.
- Transferable: useful on nearby problems, not only this instance.
- Grounded: supported by the correct trace rather than imported from a memorized template.
- Contrast-aware: inoculates against the most plausible wrong path exposed by the incorrect traces.

Failure modes to reject:
- Generic restatement of the task.
- Topic labels with no executable action.
- Hallucinated formulas or named methods not justified by the trace.
- Partial derivations that hard-code this instance.
- Advice so verbose that it becomes a worked solution.

Review checklist:
- Can a solver identify the first productive setup from this abstraction?
- Does the abstraction explain what that setup buys?
- Does it avoid locking the solver into brittle algebra?
- Does it warn against the most likely misleading shortcut when relevant?
- Would this still help on a neighboring problem from the same archetype?
